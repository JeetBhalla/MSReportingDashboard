Introduction
Welcome to the FedEx Agility Dashboard
The FedEx Agility Sprint Dashboard is a real-time reporting tool that connects directly to Agility VersionOne — the system your teams use to manage sprints and stories every day.

It is designed for:
  • Scrum Masters — track team velocity and identify carry-overs
  • Programme Managers / RTEs — monitor ART delivery rates at a glance
  • Team Leads — compare team performance within a programme
  • Sprint Review facilitators — validate pull-forward and carry-over stories with live data
Key benefit: No spreadsheets. No manual exports. Data is pulled live from Agility and refreshed on demand.

Section 1 — Sign In
Signing In with OKTA
When you first open the dashboard you'll see the login page.

Steps:
  • Enter your FedEx employee ID or email address
  • Enter your OKTA password
  • Click Sign in with OKTA
  • A browser window opens — approve the MFA push notification on your phone
  • The window closes automatically and you land on the dashboard
Your session stays active for 8 hours. On your next visit the banner will show "Existing session found" — click Go to Dashboard to skip re-login.
Tip: If you're on Streamlit Cloud, use the "Paste Session Cookie" tab — copy the cookie value from your browser's DevTools after logging in normally.

Section 2 — Filters
The Filter Bar — Your Control Panel
At the top of every dashboard page sits the filter bar. All charts and tables respond to changes here.

  • ART dropdown — Select the Agile Release Train: Conveyance, Plan & Prepare, or Run & Close. The Teams dropdown updates instantly.
  • Teams multi-select — Pick one team for a deep-dive, or select multiple teams to compare side by side. No limit on selections.
  • From / To dates — Zoom into a PI, quarter, or any custom window. Leave blank to default to the current calendar year.
  • 🔄 Refresh — Clears the cache and pulls the very latest data from Agility. Use this right after a sprint closes.
Tip: For PI review meetings, set the From/To dates to the PI start and end dates to scope all charts to that PI only.

Section 3 — Summary Cards
Headline Metrics — Instant Snapshot
Immediately after selecting your teams, five summary metric cards appear. These give you an executive-level snapshot without needing to read a single chart.

  • Selected Teams — number of teams currently in scope
  • Total Sprints — count of sprints matching your date filter
  • Planned Stories — total stories committed across all sprints
  • Delivered Stories — total stories completed
  • Overall Delivery Rate — the headline % with raw story-point totals as a delta
How to use it: In a PI planning or RTE sync, this five-number summary answers "are we on track?" before opening a single chart.

Section 4 — Tab 1
Committed vs Completed by ART
The first tab is the ART-level health check — ideal for programme and PI-level conversations.

The grouped bar chart shows:
  • ■ Purple bars — Committed stories
  • ■ Green bars — Completed stories
  • Purple % badge above each group — the delivery rate
When teams from multiple ARTs are selected, each ART gets its own bar group for cross-programme comparison. When a single ART is selected, you see that programme's aggregated numbers.
Best for: PI Planning reviews · Programme Board updates · RTE sync meetings

Section 5 — Tab 2
Team Performance — Side-by-Side Comparison
The second tab drills one level deeper — individual team performance within your selected ART.

  • Every selected team appears as its own bar group
  • Teams are sorted alphabetically
  • Delivery rate percentages are annotated above each team
  • The chart height adjusts automatically when many teams are selected
Interactive click-through: Click any team's bar in this chart and a new Sprint Velocity tab appears automatically — drilling from programme view to individual sprint detail in one click.
Tip: Hover over any bar to see the exact committed and completed counts in the tooltip before clicking through.

Section 6 — Tab 3
Sprint Velocity — Deep Dive on a Team
After clicking a team in Tab 2, the Sprint Velocity tab opens automatically.

It contains three charts:
  • Planned vs Delivered stories — classic velocity chart per sprint
  • Story Points: Planned vs Delivered — reveals complexity load changes across sprints
  • Delivery Rate % trend line — orange area chart showing consistency sprint over sprint
To return to the comparison view, click the ✖ Close button in the top-right corner of the tab.
Use in retrospectives: Dips in the delivery rate chart are great conversation starters — they prompt the team to identify what caused the drop and what to improve next sprint.

Section 7 — Sprint Table
Sprint Details Table
Below the velocity charts is the Sprint Details table — a row-by-row breakdown of every sprint.

Each row shows:
  • Sprint name and date range
  • Planned / Delivered story counts
  • Pull Forward — stories pulled in from the backlog mid-sprint after it started
  • Carry Over — stories brought in from the previous sprint because they weren't finished
  • Planned / Delivered story points
  • Delivery Rate % — colour-coded for quick scanning
Pull Forward and Carry Over show a dash for future sprints or when none occurred — keeping the table clean.
Tip: High carry-over numbers in consecutive sprints signal a capacity or dependency issue worth raising in retrospective.

Section 8 — Story Validation
Pull Forward & Carry Over Validation
For teams doing sprint reviews or retrospectives, there's an expandable audit section below the sprint table.

Click "Validate Pull Forward / Carry Over — Story Details" to expand it.

For each sprint where pull-forwards or carry-overs occurred, you see two side-by-side story tables:
  • Pull Forward stories — created after the sprint started (backlog pull-ins)
  • Carry Over stories — created before sprint start, not yet delivered
Each table shows: story ID, type (Story / Defect / Spike), title, status, created date, closed date, and estimate.
Audit trail: Cross-check these stories directly against the Agility board during sprint review for full transparency.
